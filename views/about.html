About Us

OsomTalk is an awesome chat site for your awesome team, event or LOL eaters.

We aim to be your favorite chat tool, reviving the good old times when people got together on IRC rooms to talk, have fun and work. Mixed with the features you have grown to expect in the post Twitter/Facebook era.

OsomTalk was born at HackerGarage, a HackerSpace located in Guadalajara México. And is getting the support from the fine entrepreneurial community in México (Thanks!)

Osomtalk Manifesto

A declaration of the intentions and views of the Osomtalk Team.

Simplicity

We believe that true simplicity isn't made by just hiding complex stuff under the hood, but by really making it simple for the user to grasp what is inside the product.

Users make themselves an idea of what are the rules that apply inside a system, the farther this self made ideas are from what is really happening inside, the harder it will be for them to use that system.

Because of that we try to keep the magic tricks at a minimum, even at the expense of another GUI element.

Privacy

We believe that people deserve his own personal not-tracked, not-marketed place in the Internet, a place where they can simply talk without being overheard.

We try to make OsomTalk one of this places by doing 2 things:

  1. Making totally anonymous chats.
  2. Providing the Source Code.

The second point is really important, because we don't know if in the future some silly law require us to store your data, by giving you the source code, we give you the power to make your very own place and keep your privacy untouched.

Openness

We believe that we gain more by opening the code, than by running a closed source business.

Innovation doesn't come easy, and entering an already crowded market is risky, the only thing we can do is to be really disrupting and for that we have a little mantra:

Disruptive Innovation Mantra:

  • If it doesn't exists, make it.
  • If it already exists, make it better.
  • If it is already well done, make it cheaper.
  • If it is already cheap, make it open.

Or just fuck everything and go straight to open, to create a whole new market.

By opening the code we hope a completely new market emerge, One that would eventually make a living to us and everyone in the community.

For that we have chosen the AGPLv3 license that requires that whoerever who provide a similar service using our code base, has to publish his modifications back to the community, this assure us that everyone using the code has to play nice with everyone.

Productivity

We believe productivity can be gained by giving a workforce something similar to what they use everyday.

In OsomTalk, We settled for the TimeLine Pattern (That you can see on Facebook, Twitter and Google Plus)

It has at least this elements:

  • New interactions arriving from the top.
  • A dedicated button to say that something is ok (Like, Favorite, +1).
  • Images and videos embedded directly in the page.

Its familiarity to everyone in the Internet, Make it easy for a workforce to understand the tool and start making an productive use out of it in a couple of minutes.
